Trimble's structural detailing powerhouse. Best for rebar cages, connections, BBS generation. LOD 350–400.

Structural analysis software. Provides design forces, moments, reactions for rebar design. Links to Revit via API.
🔍

Autodesk's clash detection and model coordination tool. Used to find hard and clearance clashes between rebar and MEP.
📊
AutoCAD
2D drafting for structural drawings, reinforcement details. Still widely used for shop drawings and GA layouts.

ISO 16739 open standard for model exchange. IfcReinforcingBar, IfcBeam, IfcColumn — vendor-neutral BIM sharing.
🏭
BVBS / CNC
Digital rebar fabrication format exported from Tekla to CNC bending machines. LOD 400 key deliverable — zero manual re-entry.
☁️

Autodesk cloud platform for model coordination, issue tracking, document management on live projects.

🏗️ Beam Reinforcement — Complete Guide
Beam Reinforcement Principles
A reinforced concrete beam carries loads primarily through bending. The bottom zone develops tension (carried by steel), while the top zone carries compression (concrete). Shear is resisted by diagonal compression + stirrups.
Tension zone: As = M / (0.87 fy × jd)
Shear: Vus = 0.87 fy × Asv × d / sv
Dev. length: Ld ≈ 40–50 × db (Fe500, M25)
Key Detailing Rules
  • Clear cover 25mm (internal), 40mm (external)
  • Min. 2 bars, max spacing 300mm or 0.75d
  • Stirrups @ d/2 for standard, d/4 in seismic zones
  • Stagger laps — max 50% at any section
  • Curtail bars where no longer required by BMD
  • Top bars at supports ≥ 0.25 × span length

🏛️ Column Reinforcement — Complete Guide
Column Reinforcement Principles
Columns are compression members carrying axial load + bending. Longitudinal bars carry combined axial + bending; lateral ties provide confinement, prevent bar buckling, and resist shear. Seismic design adds 135° closed ties.
As,min = 0.8% Ag (IS 456)
As,max = 6% Ag (IS 456)
Tie spacing: min(300mm, least dim., 16×db)
Seismic: min(100mm, b/4, 6×db)
Key Detailing Rules
  • Minimum 4 bars for rectangular, 6 for circular
  • 40mm clear cover to outer tie
  • 135° hooks on all tie ends (seismic zones)
  • No lap joints in joint zone or plastic hinge region
  • Lap offset: above floor level by min. 600mm
  • Cross-ties for bars spaced >150mm apart

🟫 Slab Reinforcement — Complete Guide
Two-way slabs distribute loads in both directions. The shorter span carries more load. Main bars run parallel to the shorter span at bottom; distribution bars run at right angles. Top bars are added over supports.
One-way: As,min = 0.12% bD (Fe500) — if Lx/Ly > 2
Two-way: design by BM coefficients (IS 456 Table 26)
Max bar spacing = min(3d, 300mm) for main bars
  • Main bars: T10–T16 @ 150–200mm (short span, bottom)
  • Distribution bars: T8–T10 @ 200–300mm (long span)
  • Top bars at continuous edges: 0.3× span from support
  • Edge strips: provide nominal T8@200mm each way
  • 20mm cover for internal mild exposure
  • Crank bars at 1/5 span from support face
🔩 Steel Frame Systems — Complete Guide
Structural steel frames use hot-rolled or cold-formed sections in various configurations. Frame behaviour depends on connection type — pinned, semi-rigid, or rigid moment connections determine load path and sway resistance.
Moment frame: M = wL²/8 (fixed-fixed = wL²/12)
Section classification: d/t ratios (compact, non-compact)
Slenderness: λ = Le/ry → buckling check IS 800
  • Universal Beams (UB): bending members — wide flange for stiffness
  • Universal Columns (UC): stocky, good about both axes
  • SHS/RHS: columns and braces — efficient closed sections
  • Braced frames resist lateral via diagonal braces (pinned joints)
  • Moment frames resist sway through rigid beam-column joints
  • Tekla models connections with parametric joint components

⚙️ Steel Connections — Complete Guide
Steel connections transfer forces between members. The connection type determines structural behaviour — pinned connections (shear only), moment connections (full fixity). Connections are designed for shear, axial, and moment capacity.
Bolt shear: Vds = fu × Anb / (√3 × γmb)
Weld: Lw,min = 4 × weld size (throat)
End plate: tp ≥ M / (0.9 fy × bolt lever arm)
  • Fin plate: simple shear connection — beam-to-column web
  • Flexible end plate: semi-rigid — limited moment capacity
  • Extended end plate: full moment connection — most common
  • HSFG bolts Gr.8.8 / 10.9 — high strength friction grip
  • Fillet weld: toe to heel, 6–12mm typical size
  • Stiffeners added when column web insufficient in bending
📏 Steel Section Properties — Complete Guide
Steel section properties determine strength, stiffness, and buckling resistance. In Revit and Tekla, sections are selected from standard libraries (IS 808, BS EN 10034, AISC) and assigned to structural members with material grade.
Second moment: I = bd³/12 (approx for I-section)
Section modulus: Z = I / ymax
Radius of gyration: r = √(I / A)
